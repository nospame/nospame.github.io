Origin Story

Originally from Seattle, naturally I worked at a career in coffee for close to a decade. I loved the environment, the people, and the coffee, but financially it was unsustainable. I tried moving across the country (twice), worked as a cook, helped manage a chocolate shop, and considered moving back to my hometown again.

I found a job in state government: a basic office job, I thought. As part of a remarkably small team, I helped build up the state's interagency homelessness council, working with leadership pushing for policy change and expanded funding, and answered every question imaginable as our staff grew nearly tenfold. I supported our growing grants division by stepping into a vacant analyst role, pushed the state's first ever homelessness action plan over the finish line, and earned myself an analyst position for real.

In the new role, I was again part of a small team — we produced the council's meetings, worked with our counterparts in other state departments, and wrote everything from budget letters to 70-page progress reports. Despite a great environment, I found myself drawn to the technical side of things — “What data do we have or what can we get? How do we present that information? What else can we do with it?” I am the rare one who genuinely enjoys Excel.

After much research, I enrolled in a full-time coding bootcamp, understanding these programs can give a solid foundation, but that the commitment to learning would have to last much longer than twelve weeks.

I am building a career in software development that I am just at the beginning of, recognizing now that it is so much more than “sitting in front of a computer all day”. As someone who enjoys a new adventure, has an eye for the details, and loves finding solutions to problems you only half-realized were there, I am truly excited to see where this field leads.
